Pre-Algebra Formulas and Explanations

PEMDAS (Order of Operations)
Parentheses → Exponents → Multiplication/Division → Addition/Subtraction
Example: Solve 3 + 6 × (5 + 4) ÷ 32 = ?
Properties of Addition & Multiplication
Commutative, Associative, Distributive Properties
Examples:
- Commutative: a + b = b + a
- Associative: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
- Distributive: a(b + c) = ab + ac
Integer Rules
- Same signs: Add and keep the sign
- Different signs: Subtract and keep the sign of the larger number
Fractions
Add/Subtract: Common denominator needed
Multiply: Multiply across
Divide: Flip the second fraction and multiply
Example: (2/3) ÷ (4/5) = (2/3) × (5/4) = 10/12 = 5/6
Decimals
Line up the decimal points to add or subtract.
Example: 3.45 + 6.7 = 10.15
GCF (Greatest Common Factor)
The largest factor shared by two or more numbers.
Example: GCF of 12 and 18 is 6.
LCM (Least Common Multiple)
The smallest multiple shared by two or more numbers.
Example: LCM of 4 and 5 is 20.
Percent Formula
Percent = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
Example: What percent is 30 of 60? (30 ÷ 60) × 100 = 50%
Simple Interest Formula
Interest = Principal × Rate × Time
Example: Invest $800 at 5% for 2 years: ➔ Interest = 800 × 0.05 × 2 = $80
Total Amount (Simple Interest)
Total = Principal + Interest
Example: Principal = $800, Interest = $80 ➔ Total = 800 + 80 = $880
Compound Interest Formula
A = P(1 + r/n)nt
Where:
- A = Final amount
- P = Principal
- r = Annual rate (decimal)
- n = Number of times compounded/year
- t = Years

Example: $500 at 6% for 3 years compounded annually: ➔ A = 500(1.06)3 ≈ $595.51
Compound Interest Earned
Compound Interest = Final Amount - Principal
Example: Final Amount = $595.51, Principal = $500 ➔ Compound Interest = 595.51 - 500 = $95.51
